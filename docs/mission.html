Our Mission

Our groundbreaking research addresses a critical yet overlooked challenge in space exploration: understanding menstrual fluid mechanics in zero-gravity environments. Using parabolic flight trials, we will systematically investigate how four popular tampon brands disperse fluids when removed in microgravity conditions.

By deploying high-resolution camera technology and a synthetic vaginal model (syngina), our team will capture and analyze droplet behavior, providing the first comprehensive study of menstrual product performance outside Earth's gravitational influence. Our project goes beyond technical innovation; it challenges decades of systemic under-research in women's health, particularly in extreme environments like space. From NASA's infamous 1983 tampon miscalculation to current space exploration strategies, we're bridging a critical knowledge gap that impacts astronaut well-being and mission preparedness.
